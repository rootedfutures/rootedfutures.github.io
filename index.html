The Challenge

The unseen struggles of the Rajang River

Deep within the longhouses of Song, Sarawak, infrastructure gaps threaten local ecosystems and the livelihoods of rural families.

01

Forced Pollution

With no formal waste management systems, residents are pushed toward openly burning plastic waste or dumping it into the Rajang River.

02

Heartbreaking Food Waste

Seasonal native crops often rot before reaching market because remote, off-grid longhouses cannot rely on refrigeration.

03

Systemic Marginalization

Low-income households, single-parent families, and undocumented residents face barriers to formal employment and stable income.

Our Solution

The dual-impact circular economy

Rooted Futures turns local liabilities into high-margin assets through two connected loops: industrial renewal and agricultural innovation.

Waste-to-Craft Loop

Industrial Renewal

  1. Incentivized Collection Cash-for-Trash networks reward residents for collecting river-bound plastics and e-waste.
  2. Eco-Material Engineering Collected plastic is safely processed into durable faux-rattan strips and beads.
  3. Indigenous Upcycling Artisans weave recycled materials into premium fashion items with contemporary Dayak-inspired geometry.
Surplus-to-Feast Loop

Agricultural Innovation

  1. Source Interception Volatile agricultural surplus is purchased directly from indigenous farmers before it goes to waste.
  2. Off-Grid Preservation Energy-efficient food technology preserves harvests on site without grid electricity.
  3. Premium Market Penetration Wild shoots, Dabai fruit, and seasonal delicacies become shelf-stable condiments and gourmet dry goods.
